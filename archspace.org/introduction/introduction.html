PROLOGUE
 

"...It was believed until now that the Empire, which took 3000 years to fall into decline, would take another 3000 years to complete its downfall. Technology was slowly taking a step backward, but travel at the speed of light was still remembered. Though the Empire's reins were becoming lax over its outer colonies, the Empire still possessed the ability to exercise control and influence over its realms. In the central controls of each planet, there were directors who still carried out the Emperor's wishes, and the center of the Empire, which contained the Imperial Palace, was firmly under the control of the Empire. Those who dared to challenge the Empire faced the wrath of the Imperial Armada, which still retained its fame and might. The League of Supremacy, established as an alliance between the different species of the galaxy, also had quite a bit of success in mediating differences between outer colonies and the Empire."

"The regression and slow decline of the Empire, which was gnawing away at its power like a virus, could be considered a massive tragedy when each individual battle is considered. But viewing this decay from a comprehensive viewpoint, the internal conflicts, and the devastation and ruin that resulted from these conflicts, can be seen as quite normal on the whole time line. It even became a trend among historians to calculate when the "last emperor" would reign, and when that reign would end. Like the doctor who calculates the time of death for a patient who looks fine but is battling a deadly disease and is slowly withering away..."

"It was then that a historic event occurred, an event never before seen in the entire history of all the empires that had enjoyed hegemony throughout the flow of time in the galaxy. 3 species from distant galaxies sought refuge in the Empire's galaxy. They did not directly challenge the rule of the Empire, but they settled without permission on many planets. The Empire showed hesitation in acting against these refugees."

"The three species called Xeloss, Xerusian, and Xesperados boldly shook the still equilibrium of the galaxy and interrupted the quiet decline of the Empire. Having left their home galaxy for foreign lands, these species were brimming with energy to change and create new cultures. This became the catalyst for outer planets that had sought to break away from the Empire. Unfortunately, these species, which had already been rivals in their own galaxies, also brought with them the fire of battle..."

 

--- J. C. Whitecrow, "End of the History"
