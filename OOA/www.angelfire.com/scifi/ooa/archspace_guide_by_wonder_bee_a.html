Choosing a Race:


Alright, some of you have ask about racial modifiers and best charractors, below is a listing of each races modifiers and a brief explaination of them:

Bosalians Military -2, Diplomacy+4

Xesperados Genius +3, Facility Cost -5, Spy -3, Research +2

All right, now these are all right as far as I can remember (servers down so I can't check). Now I will attempt to describe what all these mean.

Military = How fast you can build ships
Commerce = How much money is made through trade (I'll explain more later)
Enviroment = How fast it takes you to terraform
Effeciency = how fast you can build new building ig: Factory's, Military bases
Growth = How fast your population grows
Prouduction = How much money each of your factory's and bases put out
Faculty Cost = How much you'll have to pay in upkeep for your building
Spy = How often your spy mission will work
Genious = How much Exp. your commanders get from battle
Diplomacy = I think it has to do with the empire but I'm not sure
Research = How fast you can research

Now, when you choose a race, you should choose one that will be good in the long run, as you can see Targoids have good bonuses in Faculty Cost, Growth and production. Now what this enables you to do is grow very quickly and therefore your able to recover from loses of planets faster.

No one race is dominant in the game, however as of now you see a lot of people as Targoids for the reasons listed above. I myself am Xerusian because I thought the Description was cool :-P


Key Terms:

Now here I will discuss some of the terms I may use throughout this little guide that some people may not know. If you do know then skip past this part.

Terraforming = Changing your planet from Unsuitable to Suitable.
a. Now when you get a new planet from either expeditions or sieges, you will have to terraform this planet, unless you attacked the same race as you. That is the smart thing to do when first starting out.
b. Now to speed up terraforming there are only two things you can do, Invest in the planet or Get higher tech's

PP= PP is what your factory's give you each turn, the more factory's you have the more PP you get on that planet.
a. Check planet descriptions on next topic to find out more.
MP= MP is what your Military bases give you per turn, your MP takes care of fleet missions and Ship upkeep. If you exceed your MP per turn with expenses then you will make up the difference with PP. You pay 20 PP per 1MP you are over.

Commerce =As stated above commerce is your trade money you make per turn. You make this through relations through your planets and allied planets.

Waste Ratio = Now this is the one that people get pissed at, Waste Ratio effects your planet production. E.g.: if you have 50% waste then 50% of your production is disregarded and you only make 50% of what you would without waste. Each race has a different planet max without waste. This means that as a Xerusian I can have 20 planets with 0 waste and every planet I get past 20 has 2-3% waste added to it.

Sortie = When your fleets are on there way back from a cancelled mission

Buildings:
*note, if you have a modifier from one of the above you will be able to grow in that area by building more of that area. What that means is if you have a Huge artifact which is +20 on research, you will be able to build research labs really fast on that planet. Keep that in mind for the next section.

Now everyone has a different opinion on what do build on what type of planets. I myself like to do this.

Low waste and good planets such as Normal, Rich or Ultra Rich, I like to build mostly factory's, I set the ratio from anywhere to 70% Factory's 30% military bases to 90% Factory's. I set 100% factories on my big money makes though.

One my poor and ultra poors that I get stuck with in my low waste rate area I like to build mostly military bases. This is because they won't produce much PP and so instead of having them go to waste I gain some MP from them.

There is a limit to how many building can be on each planet, now I'm not sure of the exact number, but I do know it depends on the size and it's resources. For a Normal Normal I thinks it's somewhere between 1200-1500 buildings, so watch what you build.

As stated above Factory's give you PP that's your money that you can spend, you can invest this into anything from Planets to Researching to Shipbuilding. When you invest into something it speeds it up about 40% so it's a good thing to do.

Military bases give you MP with will take care of upkeep on ships you have in fleets and protect your planets from raids.

Research Labs speed up the time it takes to research a tech. You gain RP with this, but don't worry about it you don't need it once done researching.

Concentration Mode:

This is the mode that you can set, you can choose from:
Industry, which will give you more PP per turn and buildings seem to go up faster
Research, which most people are in to start the game
Military, which will invest more money into ship building which will speed that up
Balanced which will be an all around setting.


Researching:

When you start to research, you want to make sure you are building mostly research labs on your planets and also make sure you keep investing into it, this will speed it up. Most people like to be in research mode when they start the game because it's really a race to finish it. While researching however you want to make sure you are in free research because if you single out a specific tech you want it will take twice as long. Only single one out if it's one of your last important techs. Other than that it's like anything else just research!

Projects:


Now there are projects that you will be able to buy with a certain level of tech's, these projects will modify your racial modifiers discussed above. It tells you how it's going to modify it before you buy it. It's a good idea to wait to buy the projects that hurt your researching until you are done researching.There are also council projects that will help you even more.

One note I should add on projects, you need the project galactic something of free commerce before you can star t getting commerce...sorry I forgot to add that earlier.

To see what your modifiers are you can check anytime by going to Domestic main then at the bottom you'll see White book here you can see what your planet modifiers are and your racial modifier.


Attacking/Battles:

Your options for attacking are as follows:

Raiding: when you raid you send 1 fleet to a person's planet and destroy building on it. If he has military bases on it then you will need to destroy all of them before being able to hit any factory's (this is why you want to watch when you build 100% factory's)

Pirating: this will send 1 fleet in to a persons planet to pillage for money, you usually don't get much so it's just good for gaining EXP for your commanders.

Blockade: This is where you send your full armada (all your fleets) to a person's planet to fight his armada (All his fleets)
* Note that with the above three you do not need to be at war with someone

Sieging: This is where you send in your fleets to take a planet from someone...You need to be at war with them for this option to work.

Allied fleets: You can send any of your allies your fleets by going to mission and dispatch too, you must be allied to them however. Once you send him your fleets they will help him protect his planets. As of right now there is a bug, which makes allied fleets invincible. This only happens when the other person has no fleets of his own and the only one defending the planet are allied. You can kill them but they come back from the dead.... Ooohhhhh scary ;-)

If someone is raiding you, you will not be able to know who it is unless you station fleets on the planets he's raiding. Same goes for Pirating!

Stationing fleets on your planets is a great idea once you are strong enough to build decent fleet at a fast rate, what this will do is protect you from being raped while you are defenseless. I'll discuss this later.

Commanders:


You gain Exp.: for your commanders with every mission you send them one, with raids they get 1000, with blockades they get more and with sieges they get even more, the max level for a commander is level 20 and with every level they go up the more ships they can command. Not much more to ad on commanders, each race's commanders have different abilities so I don't know them all. I do know that since Xerusian have a +5 Morale their commanders stay and fight even with the "Mother Ship" is destroyed.

War:

Only the speaker of a council can declare war on another council, the only thing you can do is declare war with someone within your council, you do this by going to diplomacy and send message, then you just select the type of message as declare war.

Now once you feel you have won the war, you can send one of the three messages:

Suggest Truce: Usually when your losing you send this;-) sometimes just to be nice

Demand truce: this makes the person give you money if he accepts it, it's like 60% of what he has

Demand submission: this makes him give you 90-100% of the money he has.

Now when I say money he has, I am referring to what the single person has, if this is a council message then it's referring to whats in the council safe.

Not much else to say about war, can't give everything away!

Ships:


You build ships and when a ship is build it's put into the pool, I'll discuss this in a min...you can lower the time it takes you to build ships by increasing the amount of money you make per turn. You do this by building factory's. Don't forget once you fleet these ships you will need MP to keep them.

Now when you go to ship building you'll see something like this

Max PP per turn 7,548,543
Ivested PP left 350,000,000

PP in Pool 22,344,543

Alright now I'll explain what these mean:

Max PP per turn is the amount of money that is going into building your ships per tuen, when you have invested money in it it goes a lot higher:

Invested PP left means thats how much money you have left from what you invested into ship building

PP in ship pool is how much money has been spent on building a certian ship eg: you see right now it's at 22,344,543 that means that I'm building a DoomStar and it is almost done because Dooms take 23,716,000. So when it get's to 23,716,000 my doom star will be done and it will start to build the next one.

I'm not going to give you any ship designs because you need to learn that on your own.

Ship pool:

When you have buildt a ship it goes into the pool, and you can form fleets with these ships that are in the pool. So if I have 47 DoomStars of the same design I can put them into a fleet. You can only put ships of the same design in a fleet...Sorry.

You can later upgrade these fleets by going under the Fleet option and selection resignment. First of all your ships need to be in the stand by mode, which means they are not on a mission. Once you have selected resignment you can either add or remove ships or if you can replace all the ships with a different class and design.

not much else to say about ship pool.


A few Stratgies:


Planet Grabbers: These are ships that you use for the purpose of grabbing planets fast when someone dosen't have any ships defending himself. The consist of usually 4-6 gunboats per fleet. I like to keep 30 grabbers at all time and I station them on my home planet so they don't get destroyed in an attack. This is also a good way to get EXp for your commanders, because they gain EXP with every seige they do.

Stationed fleets: I like to station 1 fleet on every one of my planets, however since I can't afford the upkeep of 200 fleets I usually only station them on planets peolple would want. I do this because if you become defenseless you are open to get raped (all you planets taken) and if your going against a decent council you will get raped. I station usually dreads because planet grabbers are usually gun boats you fleet of dreads will fight them off and make the person seiging you use a good fleet and that take a while for them to retun....you have a fighting chance of survival that way.

Fluffing: This is dangours but sometimes nessacery. Fluffing means false power, you get this by building mass guboats and keeping them in your pool. This will inflate your power and you can either hide from people or jump up to hit them. I do it at times becasue it gives you options. I have to high of power to do it now, but if you are in the 50K to 200K range it can work for you. If you keep a fluffed power then you can hit people and if you are losing you can deflate so they can't hit you, or you can fluff up higher to avoid getting hit.

Well, I think this should be able to help most newbies out, I covered most of the things you need to get started. There is too much stratigie in this game for me to give out and plus some things you need to learn on your own. Hope you enjoyed.
